Card Issuing & Self-Acquiring Platform

Took Pesapal from payments processor to full-stack financial infrastructure provider — owning the entire card lifecycle from issuance to settlement.

Read case study+

The challenge

Pesapal was entirely reliant on third-party issuers and acquirers — meaning high transaction costs, limited data visibility, and a fragmented customer experience across Openfloat, Drive, and Credit products. Every new feature required wrangling an external partner. Innovation was being outsourced.

What I built

Payments · Regulatory Partnerships

02

Scaling MOMO Payments via Interoperable QR (PayQR)

When Tanzania's TanQR standard arrived, only banks could issue QR codes. I found the partnership model that let a fintech win anyway.

1 QR Accepts all MOMO providers — Tigo, Vodacom, and beyond
Read case study+

The challenge

Merchants in Tanzania could only accept MOMO payments through direct integrations with Tigo and Vodacom. When the Bank of Tanzania introduced TanQR — a universal interoperability standard — it was a huge opportunity. But there was a hard catch: only banks could issue QR codes. Fintechs were locked out by regulation.

The solution: partner, don't build around

Rather than fight the regulatory wall, I designed a bank partnership model that preserved the fintech's merchant ownership while leveraging the bank's regulatory standing.

Merchant KYC shared with Mwangaza Bank → Bank issues TanQR tied to master account → QR mapped to merchants → Customer scans → pays via any MOMO → funds flow Bank → Fintech → Merchant.

What I owned

  • Defined product strategy to leverage TanQR within regulatory constraints
  • Led the bank partnership and integration model design
  • Designed end-to-end payment and merchant onboarding flows
  • Scoped QR management, reconciliation, and notification systems

Impact

MOMO provider coverage with one QR code
↓ 0

Merchant growth from higher payment success

Platform · Operations

03

Reserveport Restaurant — Unified Delivery & Operations Platform

The pandemic forced restaurants into multi-tablet chaos. I reframed the problem and built the coordination layer they actually needed.

1 Dashboard replacing Uber Eats, Bolt Food & Jumia Food management silos
Read case study+

The context

COVID-19 collapsed dine-in revenue overnight. Restaurants scrambled onto every third-party delivery platform simultaneously — drowning in multi-tablet fatigue with no inventory sync between Uber Eats, Bolt Food, and Jumia Foods. Stock inconsistencies, high cancellation rates, and overwhelmed kitchen staff became the daily reality.

The insight that changed the product

"The real product wasn't another delivery app — it was a single source of truth for restaurant operations across all delivery platforms."

The strategic trade-off

We evaluated three paths: standalone delivery app, proprietary fleet, or a unified order and inventory orchestration layer. We chose the third — deliberately skipping a delivery fleet to maximize coverage and minimize overhead, integrating existing aggregators instead.

What we shipped

  • Consumer experience — unified ordering interface, real-time menus, voucher engine to drive affordability during economic downturn
  • Merchant command center — real-time inventory sync across all platforms, unified order management, automated stock updates via API

Key learning

The true product was never food delivery — it was coordination infrastructure for fragmented ecosystems. Shipping during crisis required accepting controlled technical debt, but we got to market when restaurants needed us most.

Apache Superset Upgrade — Analytics Platform Modernization

A v1-to-v5 platform upgrade spanning 4 workstreams and the entire analytics org. Not just an engineering task — a product transformation.

Credit · Automation · Fintech

08

Automated CRB Check on Loan Tab

Manual CRB checks were slowing down merchant lending, inflating costs, and creating inconsistent customer experiences. I designed a smart, event-driven credit assessment system that balanced speed, compliance, and cost discipline.

1 call Per merchant per 30-day window — maximum CRB API cost cap enforced by design
Read case study+

The problem

At Pesapal, creditworthiness checks through CRB providers were handled manually — creating a slow, expensive, and inconsistent experience on both sides of the lending relationship.

  • Merchant side — delayed eligibility feedback, no transparency on qualification status, friction from manual review processes
  • Business side — high operational overhead, repeated CRB API costs, inconsistent auditability, slow loan funnel conversion

My role

  • Led product discovery and requirements definition
  • Designed business rules with compliance and credit teams
  • Coordinated API workflow with engineering
  • Defined the cost optimization strategy for CRB provider usage
  • Drove rollout planning and cross-functional stakeholder alignment

Product strategy

"Balance speed, compliance, and operational cost efficiency — don't just automate, design smart."

Three core product decisions shaped the solution:

  • Trigger on user intent only — CRB checks fire only when a merchant clicks the Loan tab or applies for credit, not on a blanket schedule. Eliminates unnecessary provider calls.
  • 30-day result caching — stored CRB status reused within 30 days; new check triggered only when stale. Balances compliance freshness with API spend.
  • Opt-in driven eligibility — checks only run for merchants who explicitly opted into Pesapal Credit, aligning with consent and data privacy requirements.

Business rules implemented

  • Opt-in only — checks run only for opted-in merchants
  • First access trigger — initial CRB check on first loan interaction
  • 30-day validity window — stored result reused within the window
  • Action-based refresh — refresh only when merchant re-engages
  • No idle processing — zero blanket background CRB refreshes
  • Audit logging — every CRB response stored with full metadata
  • Cost cap — maximum 1 CRB call per merchant per 30-day window

Technical trade-offs

  • Cached CRB results — reduced costs vs slightly older data (acceptable within 30-day compliance window)
  • On-demand refresh — lower infrastructure load vs delayed refresh for inactive users
  • Event-driven triggers — efficient system activity aligned with actual business intent

Impact

Instant
Merchant eligibility feedback at loan tab
↓ Cost
Reduced redundant CRB provider API calls
↓ Ops
Manual CRB processing workload eliminated
100%
Audit coverage — every CRB response logged

Key learnings

  • Automation alone is not enough — the real value came from smart refresh logic, not simply automating every CRB request
  • Cost-aware product design matters in fintech — third-party integrations scale costs quickly; billing models must be considered at the product design stage
  • Event-driven systems improve efficiency — triggering only on meaningful merchant actions reduced waste and aligned system activity with real business intent
